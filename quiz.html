Round 2: Local Trivia - Wandsworth Trivia Quiz

  1. What is the origin of the name "Wandsworth"?
    A) Named after a famous historical figure
    B) Named after the River Wandle
    C) Derived from an old English term for a market town
    D) Named after a local family estate
  2. Which of these is a notable event or idea from Wandsworth's history?
    A) The Great Fire of Wandsworth
    B) The Putney Debates
    C) The signing of the Magna Carta
    D) The first public railway in the world
  3. How many bars are located along Garrett Lane alone?
    A) 5
    B) 14
    C) 20
    D) 3
  4. What significant structure in Wandsworth was once used as a House of Correction?
    A) Wandsworth Prison
    B) The Old Town Hall
    C) The Ram Brewery
    D) The Wandle Industrial Museum
  5. Which famous London bridge connects Wandsworth to Fulham?
    A) Battersea Bridge
    B) Putney Bridge
    C) Wandsworth Bridge
    D) Hammersmith Bridge
  6. In which area of Wandsworth would you find the first purpose-built mosque in London?
    A) Clapham Junction
    B) Southfields
    C) Tooting
    D) Balham
  7. Which green space in Wandsworth was once a site of common land and now contains some of London's most expensive townhouses?
    A) Battersea Park
    B) Wandsworth Common
    C) Tooting Bec Common
    D) Clapham Common
  8. Which of these famous personalities learned their trade at a restaurant in Wandsworth?
    A) Jamie Oliver
    B) Gordon Ramsay
    C) Heston Blumenthal
    D) Nigella Lawson
  9. What was the original function of the Surrey Iron Railway, located in Wandsworth?
    A) Passenger transport
    B) Transporting coal and other goods
    C) A military railway
    D) Transporting mail and express parcels
  10. The Ram Brewery in Wandsworth is notable for being?
    A) The oldest brewery in London
    B) The first to brew lager in the UK
    C) The site of the first ale pub in England
    D) All of the above

Answers:

  • 1. B) Named after the River Wandle
  • 2. B) The Putney Debates
  • 3. B) 14
  • 4. A) Wandsworth Prison
  • 5. C) Wandsworth Bridge
  • 6. B) Southfields
  • 7. B) Wandsworth Common
  • 8. B) Gordon Ramsay
  • 9. B) Transporting coal and other goods
  • 10. A) The oldest brewery in London

Round 3: General Knowledge

  1. What is the first Prime Number?
  2. What is the name of the world's oldest known musical instrument?
  3. Which famous scientist's last words were lost to history because his nurse did not understand German?
  4. How long is a marathon?
  5. Muhammad Ali, after refusing to be drafted into the Vietnam war, was banned from boxing for how many years?
  6. Which actor portrayed Rocky Balboa in the film series Rocky?
  7. Which vegetable was originally carved before the pumpkin during Halloween traditions in its country of origin?
  8. Who is the author of To Kill a Mockingbird?
  9. What number is the Roman numeral "C"?
  10. How many teeth does an adult human typically have?

Answers:

  • 1. 2
  • 2. Flute (made from a vulture bone, discovered in Germany, dating back 35,000 years)
  • 3. Albert Einstein
  • 4. 26.2 miles
  • 5. 3 years
  • 6. Sylvester Stallone
  • 7. Turnips (beets and potatoes also accepted)
  • 8. Harper Lee
  • 9. 100
  • 10. 32
